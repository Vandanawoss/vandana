Dr. Vandana Singh is an academic leader with extensive experience in transdisciplinary program development and collaborative curriculum innovation. At the University of Tennessee, Knoxville (UTK), she leads the design and implementation of high-impact undergraduate and graduate programs spanning multiple academic disciplines and aligned with rapidly evolving industry needs. Dr. Singh’s leadership has resulted in the successful development and Tennessee Higher Education Commission (THEC) approval of five undergraduate degrees—Applied Artificial Intelligence, Data Science, Cybersecurity, Innovative Transdisciplinary Studies (ITS), and Information Sciences—and two graduate programs in Sustainability and Data Science. In her role as Program Director and the Assistant Dean for Faculty Affairs and New Programs at the College of Emerging and Collaborative Studies (CECS), Dr. Singh facilitated the engagement of over 100 faculty members and a dozen university-wide committees, working closely with department heads, associate deans, and industry partners to advance CECS’s academic mission.

Prior to joining CECS, Dr. Singh spent over two decades conducting internationally recognized interdisciplinary research and preparing professionals in information science and technology. Her global research collaborations span computer science, engineering, nursing, education, business information systems, and the arts and sciences, and include participation in international teams with members from up to ten countries. Dr. Singh’s work reflects a career-long commitment to academic innovation, global engagement, and the power of collective effort.

Currently, Dr. Singh is a Professor at the iSchool, the School of Information Sciences (SIS) at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville. In 2022-2023, Dr. Singh served as the founding Director of Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion at the College of Communication and Information. Before that, Dr. Singh was the founding Director of Undergraduate Studies (2017 – 2019) at the School of Information Sciences (SIS) at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville.

About Me: Details
======
Dr. Singh joined the SIS faculty as an Assistant Professor in 2008 and was tenured and promoted to Associate Professor in 2014 and full Professor in 2022. In her time at SIS, Dr. Singh has worked as Information Science and Technology Minor Coordinator (2008 – 2011), Acting Associate Director of SIS (2013), and the inaugural Director of Undergraduate Studies (May 2017 – April 2019).  

Dr. Singh graduated with a Ph.D. in Information Science from the iSchool at the  University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, USA, in  2008. Her dissertation research was on Online Communities to support the Users of Open Source Software. For more details about her research, please click here. She holds two master’s degrees, one in Computer Science from the University of Chicago, Illinois, USA, and the other in Knowledge Management Systems from Wageningen University, the Netherlands. Her undergraduate degree is from G.B. Pant University, in the foothills of the Himalayas in India. Please browse through the site to get to know Dr. Singh 🙂

Her current research interests are in AI Ethics, AI Education, AI Literacy, Gender and IT, Open Source Software, Women in STEM, Rural Libraries, Online Learning/Communities, and UX & Computer Supported Cooperative Work.

Dr. Singh teaches the core course in AI applications, Information Technology, Usability Testing and Methods, Web Design, Information Networking Applications, and a wide range of topics as Independent Studies. Please read more details about her teaching here.

At the College of Communication and Information, Dr. Singh has received four college-level awards in Innovative Technology Teaching, exemplary Service, and extraordinary Research achievements. Dr. Singh received an outstanding administrator award from the College of Emerging and Collaborative Studies in 2025. 

Dr. Singh has received multiple research grants from federal agencies, including the National Science Foundation (NSF), the Institute of Museum and Library Services (IMLS), and the United States Geological Society (USGS) and Google. Her research has been published, recognized, and awarded in national and international conferences and journals. Please read more details about her research here.
